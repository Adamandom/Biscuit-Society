Biscuit Olympics!

Posted on 12/1/20

It was a tough day, but in the end we won second place in the world's first Biscuit Olympics. our little trooper was a Wagon Wheel. He breezed through the 400m, 800m and 1500m events... not that great for the high jump, mind you.

At the end of the day, we left with 6 gold coins - not too shabby for a first attempt!

A Robotic Wonder!

Posted on 2/11/19

We at the Biscuit Society had the pleasure of witnessing the construction of the first ever robotic biscuit! Researchers at the Harvard robotics institute of robotics called us over to witness the event and we just couldn't decline. The robot was capable of playing sax - tenor and alto.

Young Billy, a roboticist prodigy, had proposed his idea and saw it through with a team of undergraduates. when asked, he said his goal was to "prove that Ska is not dead". After it's performance, we believe his point was proven.
